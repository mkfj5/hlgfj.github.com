01. Lesson Plan - Week 12

# Lesson Plan: Week 12

This week we'll continue talking about probabilistic graphical models by introducing hidden Markov models, which are used to model sequences (including time-series data). This lesson is closely related to final project where you'll build a part of speech tagger with HMMs. HMMs have been successfully used on both supervised and unsupervised problems with sequence data in applications like speech recognition, bioinformatics, sign language recognition, and gesture recognition.

Speech & Language Processing Chapter 4 & Chapter 9 are useful for the last project.

Classroom

  • Watch Hidden Markov Models
  • (Optional) Watch Advanced HMMs lessons (under "Extracurricular")
  • Complete the last project: part of speech tagging. In this project you'll use a hidden Markov model to tag parts of speech in English sentences, which is a common pre-processing task in natural language processing.

Reading

  • AIMA: Chapter 15.1-15.3
  • Speech & Language Processing Chapter 9 and Chapter 10
  • (Optional) The Hidden Markov Model Toolkit (HTK)
  • (Optional) Chapter 1 The Fundamentals of HTK (pages 3-13) in The HTK Book (version 3.4) [PDF | HTML].
